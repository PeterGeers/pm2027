Fh-dce pres meeting



The FH-DCE Presidents Meeting is a meeting of the presidents (club leaders) of Harley-Davidson clubs that belong to the Federation of Harley‑Davidson Clubs of Europe (FH-DCE). 🏍️



What FH-DCE is

The Federation of Harley‑Davidson Clubs of Europe is a large federation of independent Harley-Davidson clubs across Europe with tens of thousands of members. It coordinates activities between clubs and organizes major events like the FH‑DCE Super Rally.



What the Presidents Meeting is

The FH-DCE Presidents Meeting is a periodic meeting where:



Presidents of the member clubs meet each other



Federation decisions and plans are discussed



Upcoming rallies and events are coordinated



Club issues, rules, and cooperation between countries are addressed



Think of it as the “board meeting” of the European Harley club network. 🏍️⚙️



Who attends

Usually:



Club presidents (one per club)



FH-DCE board members



Sometimes organizers of upcoming rallies or events



Example

For instance, a Presidents Meeting has been held in places like Vianen in the Netherlands, where club leaders gather for discussions and networking.



💡 In practice, it’s not a public biker rally. It’s more of a closed organizational meeting for club leadership, although it often includes social activities and riding.

Behind the scenes



The official agenda of the FH-DCE Presidents Meeting is neat and tidy. Minutes, votes, reports. But the real story usually happens in the hallways, hotel bar corners, and late-night tables covered in empty glasses and patches. 🏍️🍺



Here are some of the typical behind-the-scenes dynamics people in the Harley club scene talk about.



🧭 Lobbying before the votes

Before anything is voted on, presidents often spend the evening before the meeting talking with other club leaders.



Why?



Convincing clubs to support a proposal



Gaining backing to host a future rally



Building alliances between clubs from different countries



It’s a bit like a mini European parliament… but with leather vests instead of suits.



🌍 The Super Rally politics

One of the most valuable things discussed is the right to organize the FH‑DCE Super Rally.



Hosting it means:



Thousands of bikers



Big prestige for the club



Significant revenue for the organizing club



Because of that, clubs often:



Present detailed proposals



Privately ask other presidents for support



Promote their location as the best choice



Sometimes the lobbying starts a year or two before the vote.



🤝 Club alliances

Many clubs have long-standing friendships with clubs from other countries.



Examples:



Scandinavian clubs supporting each other



German and Dutch clubs cooperating



Smaller clubs aligning with larger ones for influence



These informal alliances often determine how votes go.



⚖️ Membership debates

Occasionally the meeting discusses clubs applying to join the federation.



Behind the scenes, presidents might discuss:



Whether the club fits FH-DCE culture



If the club is stable and well organized



Whether there are conflicts with other local clubs



These conversations rarely appear in the official minutes.



🍻 Reputation matters

In the Harley club world, reputation spreads fast.



During the social parts of the meeting people quietly exchange opinions like:



“That club runs great rallies.”



“Their organization is messy.”



“Those guys are solid people.”



Those informal judgments can influence future cooperation and votes.



🧵 Patch diplomacy

Another subtle thing: patch culture.



At meetings you’ll see jackets with:



Club patches



Rally patches



Friendship patches from other clubs



Trading patches or gifts is a small but meaningful way to build goodwill between clubs.



🌙 The late-night negotiations

The real decisions sometimes happen after midnight.



Imagine:



A quiet hotel bar



Presidents from five countries around one table



Maps, rally ideas, and promises of future support



By the time the official meeting starts the next day, many votes are already decided.



💡 Fun fact: In many cases the actual meeting itself is relatively short, because the groundwork has already been laid the night before.

Why



The split comes down to culture, structure, and independence. In the biker universe, not all Harley clubs ride under the same philosophy. Some happily join the Federation of Harley-Davidson Clubs of Europe, while others steer clear of it entirely. Here’s why.



🏛️ It’s a federation with rules

FH-DCE is a structured federation.



Member clubs agree to things like:



statutes and bylaws



voting procedures



federation policies



coordinated events such as the FH-DCE Super Rally



For many clubs that’s great because it creates order, cooperation, and large events.



But some biker clubs dislike any outside structure telling them how to operate. For them, independence is sacred.



🏍️ MC culture values autonomy

Traditional Motorcycle Clubs (MCs) often operate with a different mindset:



strict internal hierarchy



strong territorial identity



no external governing body



Joining a federation could be seen as giving up part of that autonomy. Many MCs prefer to answer only to their own club leadership.



🧭 Different traditions

FH-DCE clubs tend to be touring-oriented Harley clubs.



Typical focus:



rallies



touring rides



international friendships



family-friendly events



Many MCs historically formed around brotherhood and club identity first, not large open rallies.



So the cultures developed on parallel tracks.



⚖️ Neutral ground

Another reason: FH-DCE tries to stay neutral and inclusive across Europe.



Clubs from dozens of countries cooperate under the same umbrella. That works well for social clubs, but MCs often prefer clear, independent identities rather than federations.



🍻 Different event style

FH-DCE events like the FH-DCE Super Rally are usually:



very large



open to many riders



organized like festivals



MC events are often smaller, invitation-based gatherings focused on club relationships rather than massive attendance.



The short version

Think of it like two roads in the Harley world:



FH-DCE clubs



federation



democratic structure



huge rallies



international cooperation



Independent MCs



full autonomy



tight internal structure



smaller club-centered events



Both ride Harleys, both love the culture, but their philosophy about organization is different.

Patches



In the biker world, patches are not decoration. They are more like a passport, résumé, and flag sewn into one piece of cloth. Every stitch tells a story about identity, loyalty, and territory. 🧵🏍️



Here are the key pieces of the patch culture you’ll notice around Harley clubs and meetings connected with the Federation of Harley-Davidson Clubs of Europe.



🧥 The back patch (the “colors”)

The big patch on the back of a vest or jacket is called colors.



Typical structure:



Top rocker

Name of the club



Center patch

Club logo



Bottom rocker

Territory or country



Example layout:



\[ CLUB NAME ]

&#x20;  (LOGO)

\[ NETHERLANDS ]

For many clubs this is the most important symbol they wear.



🧩 One-piece vs three-piece patches

There are two common styles.



One-piece patch

Often used by:



Harley owners clubs



touring clubs



social riding groups



Usually a single large patch with logo and name.



These are common in FH-DCE clubs.



Three-piece patch

Used mainly by MCs (Motorcycle Clubs).



Structure:



top rocker (club name)



center logo



bottom rocker (territory)



In MC culture this style has very strong meaning and strict rules.



🌍 Territory matters

The bottom rocker often shows territory:



Examples:



Netherlands



Holland



specific region or city



In MC culture territory can be sensitive. Clubs avoid copying another club’s patch or territory to prevent conflict.



FH-DCE clubs usually operate in a friendlier touring environment, so territorial tension is much lower.



🧷 Small patches on the front

The front of a vest becomes a kind of personal timeline.



Common patches:



Name patch

Your nickname



Club position



President



Road Captain



Treasurer



Rally patches

Events you attended, like the FH-DCE Super Rally



Friendship patches

Given by other clubs as a sign of respect.



🤝 Patch trading

At big biker gatherings, patch trading is a ritual.



Two riders might exchange patches from their clubs. It’s like saying:



“Your club is welcome among ours.”



Over years, a vest can become a map of friendships across Europe.



⚠️ The unwritten rules

Some basic etiquette most bikers follow:



Never touch someone’s back patch without permission



Never copy another club’s design



Earn patches through membership or events



Don’t wear patches from clubs you’re not part of



Breaking those rules can cause serious offense.



🎭 Why people care so much

To outsiders it’s just fabric.



To bikers it represents:



loyalty



time spent riding together



club history



personal identity



A well-worn vest with faded patches is basically a biker’s autobiography stitched in thread.

🏁 HOG vs FH-DCE

1\. HOG (Harley Owners Group)

Officially backed by Harley-Davidson itself.



Worldwide network of Harley riders, organized locally into chapters.



Focuses on:



Touring and social rides



Family-friendly events



Brand loyalty to Harley



Membership usually comes with buying a Harley and paying HOG fees.



Patches:



Usually simpler, standardized HOG logo patches.



Less about club autonomy; more about showing you’re part of the official Harley community.



2\. FH-DCE

Independent European federation of Harley clubs.



Not officially run by Harley-Davidson, though closely aligned culturally.



Clubs are fully autonomous but cooperate through FH-DCE.



Focuses on:



European touring



Big multi-club rallies like Super Rally



Democratic decision-making among clubs



Patches:



Club-specific, often colorful and unique.



Show club name, country, and often position in the club.



Trading and friendship patches are a big part of the culture.



3\. Key cultural difference

HOG: Brand-driven, global, structured from the company level.



FH-DCE: Club-driven, European, federated but bottom-up.



Think of it like:



HOG is the “official Harley team jersey” 🏁



FH-DCE is the “Harley club federation league” 🏍️✨



Many riders are members of both, but HOG riders usually don’t do the politics and patch diplomacy of FH-DCE clubs, and vice versa.
